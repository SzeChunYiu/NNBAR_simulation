# §5.4 `TPC` builder (src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc)

Status: detailed.

## Builder scope and dependencies

`TPC::Construct_Volumes(mother)` constructs the reusable logical-volume
model for the time-projection chamber: one vertical Type-I aluminum wall
box with gas layer daughters and one horizontal Type-II aluminum wall box
with gas layer daughters (src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:97-161). It then
places those two logical volumes twelve times around the beampipe: six
front modules at negative z and six back modules at positive z
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:164-192).

The builder depends on beampipe globals for `Beampipe_5_len`,
`Beampipe_5_radius_2`, and `Beampipe_6_len`
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:39-43). It also declares global
`TPC_drift_len`, `TPC_wall_thickness`, and `TPC_z`, but the constructor
body shadows all three with local variables before building geometry
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:44-47,114-118). The output vector is a
file-scope `TPC_Construction_list`, not a fresh local vector; each call
pushes the two wall LVs and two gas-layer LVs into that global list
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:37,198-201,265).

## Materials and decision branches

`DefineMaterials` creates `CO2` and `Gas` without existence guards.
`Gas` is Ar/CO2 80/20 by volume, implemented as 78% Ar and 22% CO2 by
mass at 1.70 mg/cm³, 293.15 K, and 1 atm
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:49-83). The gas mean excitation energy is
set to 167 eV, and construction prints the gas density, excitation
energy, and a physics W-value note of approximately 26 eV per ion pair
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:84-94). The aluminum wall material is
retrieved as `Aluminum`, which is defined by the beampipe builder before
TPC construction in the current detector-construction order
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:106-109).

There are no runtime geometry feature branches. The repeated structures
come from two fixed `for` loops that place 1 cm gas layers across the
0.85 m drift length, so each Type-I and Type-II logical volume receives
85 gas-layer placements when `TPC_drift_len/TPC_layer_thickness` remains
0.85 m / 1 cm (src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:114-127,147-160).

## Type-I and Type-II volume hierarchy

| TPC type | Mother wall LV | Gas layer LV | Layering direction and dimensions | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Type I (vertical side modules) | `TPCLV_1`, aluminum `G4Box`, x = `TPC_drift_len + 2*wall`, y = `(2*Beampipe_5_radius_2 + 2*TPC_y_2)/2`, z = `TPC_z` | `TPC_1_layer_LV`, gas `G4Box` | 85 layers of 1 cm thickness step along x, centered from `-TPC_drift_len/2` to `+TPC_drift_len/2`; layer y and z subtract the 2 mm wall margins | (src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:129-150) |
| Type II (horizontal top/bottom modules) | `TPCLV_2`, aluminum `G4Box`, x = `2*Beampipe_5_radius_2 + 2*wall`, y = `TPC_drift_len + 2*wall`, z = `TPC_z` | `TPC_2_layer_LV`, gas `G4Box` | 85 layers of 1 cm thickness step along y, centered from `-TPC_drift_len/2` to `+TPC_drift_len/2`; layer x and z subtract the 2 mm wall margins | (src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:119-127,152-160) |

`TPC_z` is computed as `(Beampipe_5_len + 2*Beampipe_6_len)/2`, then
used as the full box z-length when passed as `TPC_z/2` to `G4Box`
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:117,143-157). The builder emits a `std::cout`
diagnostic with beampipe radius and Type-II dimensions before creating
the Type-II placements (src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:152).

## Module placements

All placements use physical volume name `TPCPV` and copy numbers 0-11.
The front ring is placed at `z = -TPC_z/2`; the back ring is placed at
`z = +TPC_z/2` (src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:164-192).

| Copy numbers | Type | Position pattern | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0, 3, 6, 9 | Type II top/bottom modules | x = 0; y = `±(Beampipe_5_radius_2 + TPC_y_2/2)`; z = front/back | (src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:165,168,173,176,180,183,187,190) |
| 1, 2, 10, 11 | Type I left-side modules | x = `-(TPC_x_2/2 + TPC_x_1/2)`; y = `±TPC_y_1/2`; z = front/back | (src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:166-167,177-178,181-182,191-192) |
| 4, 5, 7, 8 | Type I right-side modules | x = `+(TPC_x_2/2 + TPC_x_1/2)`; y = `±TPC_y_1/2`; z = front/back | (src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:169-170,174-175,184-189) |

Because the same two mother logical volumes are reused for multiple
physical placements, SD attachment to the returned LVs affects all 12
placed modules rather than distinct per-module logical volumes
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:180-201).

## Regions, cuts, user limits, and visual state

`TPC_wall_region` contains `TPCLV_1` and `TPCLV_2`, with production cuts
of 0.1 cm for gammas and 0.1 mm for electrons; the wall LVs also receive
user limits that kill tracks below 1 keV or beyond 10 ms
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:207-222). `TPC_region` contains the two gas
layer LVs, with 0.1 mm production cuts for gammas, electrons, positrons,
and protons, plus the same 1 keV / 10 ms user limits
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:224-248). All TPC wall and gas logical
volumes are made invisible for visualisation
(src/detector/TPC_geometry.cc:250-263).
