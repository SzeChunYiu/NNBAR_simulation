# Geometry truth and alignment-systematic seam

*Charter.* Lock the geometry as built today (already audited via the
the verified geometry builder files) and design a misalignment seam so
that future systematic studies can apply controlled perturbations
without touching the simulation source.

## 0.1 Wave 6 derivation — geometry truth and alignment nuisance

### Physics derivation

**What is physically measured.** Geometry truth is the map from each
placed detector volume to its material, dimensions, position, and
orientation in the global coordinate system. Alignment systematics
measure how deviations from that map bias vertex position, track
direction, photon pointing, material traversal, and background veto
acceptance. The ground-truth quantity is therefore a rigid transform
per placed volume, not a reconstructed track or calorimeter variable.

**Estimator rationale.** Geant4 geometry placement is the deterministic
estimator for the as-built nominal detector: every hit and material
step depends on the active `G4PVPlacement` transform graph
`\cite{Apostolakis1999,Agostinelli2003}`. A small-misalignment
systematic is naturally represented as a nuisance distribution on
translation and rotation transforms because first-order detector
response shifts are generated by moving the sensitive or passive
volume and replaying the same physics. Independent Gaussian widths are
a conservative v0.1 engineering prior until survey covariance matrices
exist; correlations must be added before a quoted final result.

**Statistical character.** The `perfect` geometry has no sampling
variance: for a fixed source tree and build options it is a deterministic
truth map. Alignment scenarios introduce pseudo-experiment variance
through sampled transforms, but the dominant uncertainty is systematic
because the placeholder widths may not match real survey tolerances.
The correct closure observable is therefore the induced shift in
vertex resolution, invariant-mass width, material budget, and veto
acceptance across repeated scenario seeds.

- **Alignment widths for `nominal_survey`.** Grounding:

  2026-06-22.
- **Alignment widths for `worst_case_construction`.** Grounding:

- **Independence of volume perturbations.** Grounding: v0.1 scenario

  common-mode translations/rotations and parent-child correlations
  once a survey or mechanical stack model is available; target
  resolution date 2026-06-29.
- **Geometry-audit implementation.** Grounding: §2 records that no

### Closure test for the derivation

1. Build the `perfect` geometry and dump every active placed volume's
   name, material, half-dimensions, translation, and rotation.
2. Apply each alignment scenario for a fixed seed set and verify that
   `perfect` is an exact no-op while non-perfect scenarios reproduce
   the requested Gaussian widths in millimetres and milliradians.
3. Regenerate a small signal/control sample under `perfect`,
   `nominal_survey`, and `worst_case_construction`. Compare primary
   vertex residuals, π⁰-mass width, material-map `X/X₀`, and veto
   acceptance shifts.
4. Record the scenario-induced shifts in plan 47 and propagate the
   largest defensible shift through plan 45. If the geometry dump or
   implementation seam is absent, alignment rows remain blocked rather
   than being treated as simulated systematics.

## 3. Alignment as a systematic

The current configuration is *perfect alignment* — every volume sits
at its nominal position and orientation. Real detectors carry
misalignments from survey, mounting, and operating environment.

The alignment seam plugs into the geometry just before placement
(`G4PVPlacement(rotation, translation, …)`) and applies a per-LV
perturbation drawn from a scenario file:

```yaml
# alignment_scenarios/<tag>.yml
seed: 12345
scenarios:
  Beampipe:
    translation_sigma_mm: {x: 0.2, y: 0.2, z: 0.5}
    rotation_sigma_mrad: {x: 0.1, y: 0.1, z: 0.2}
  Beampipe_Shielding:
    translation_sigma_mm: {x: 2.0, y: 2.0, z: 2.0}
    rotation_sigma_mrad: {x: 1.0, y: 1.0, z: 1.0}
  Silicon:
    translation_sigma_mm: {x: 0.05, y: 0.05, z: 0.10}
    rotation_sigma_mrad: {x: 0.10, y: 0.10, z: 0.20}
  TPC:
    translation_sigma_mm: {x: 0.5, y: 0.5, z: 1.0}
    rotation_sigma_mrad: {x: 0.3, y: 0.3, z: 0.5}
  Scintillator:
    translation_sigma_mm: {x: 1.0, y: 1.0, z: 1.0}
    rotation_sigma_mrad: {x: 0.5, y: 0.5, z: 0.5}

    translation_sigma_mm: {x: 0.5, y: 0.5, z: 0.5}
```

## 4. Implementation seam

Verified current seam point: every active builder in §1 implements a
`Construct_Volumes(G4LogicalVolume* mother)` method. No alignment helper
exists yet. The proposed implementation is a future
`nnbar::Alignment::Apply(transform, lv_name, scenario)` helper inserted
at placement time; until L3/Sim Production implements it, Plan 16 only
registers the scenario contract and does not claim source-level support.

In `perfect` mode the future helper must be a no-op (the transform is
returned unchanged), satisfying plan 02's identity-default discipline.

## 6. Risks and mitigations

- *Risk:* survey-grade alignment input is unavailable for HIBEAM
  before commissioning.
  *Mitigation:* `nominal_survey` is a placeholder driven by typical
  detector survey precision; revisited at commissioning.
- *Risk:* alignment perturbation breaks the future geometry verifier
  (positions no longer match the reference baseline).
  *Mitigation:* the verifier is run only on `perfect` configurations;
  alignment-perturbed builds are registered separately in plan 03.
